About Me

I am a PhD student at the University of Edinburgh, (specifically EPCC, the supercomputing part) having graduated from Queens University Belfasst in 2025 with a first class honours in Computer Science MEng. My project is supporting heterogenous architectures for ASiMoV-CCS, which is a CFD codebase developed at EPCC in collaboration with Rolls-Royce.

Over the past few years I have been lucky to attend the ISC HPC Conference in Hamburg in 2024 as a student volunteer, and worked as a research assistant at the Centre for Secure Information Technologies (CSIT) at Queen's University Belfast. I have an interest in HPC, novel technologies such as FPGAs and quantum computing, as well as approaches to development (formal methods and algorithm analysis).

Outside of my studies, I have a huge interest in sport (every sport you can think of), and I have also been getting into my fitness, with a goal to hit a 25 minute 5KM, and sub 2 hour marathon by the end of the year (2025). I also have a wide array of other interests like reading and writing, the humanities, and movies!
